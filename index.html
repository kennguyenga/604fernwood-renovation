Vision & Concept
Two products · one lot

The opportunity. 604 Fernwood now becomes an 8-unit STR campus on a single parcel: a 6-unit motel building of compact studio units, plus a separate 2-unit duplex created by tying the existing Unit 7 house and the existing rear shed together with a new 40-ft connector. RMF zoning permits multi-family. The L-shaped main motel + the combined duplex coexist on the 0.42 acre with their own entries and parking zones.

The motel play. Each of the 6 existing bedrooms becomes a private studio. The 3 kitchens that currently sit between pairs of units are split down the middle with new fire-rated walls — each unit ends up with its own private half-kitchen (small range, dishwasher drawer, full-size fridge, sink, counter, and a small bar with 2 stools) plus a dedicated utility room containing a stacked W/D and wall-mounted tankless water heater. The pass-through doors between units are walled in. The bathroom sits inside the bedroom as an en-suite, with a second door opening to the living/kitchen side so common-area guests can access it without entering the bedroom. Zero shared access between units.

The duplex play. Existing Unit 7 (19'×23' shell) and the existing shed (24'×16') are kept and tied together with a new 40'×23' connector — total ~83'×23' building, split as a mirrored side-by-side duplex. Each unit is ~955 sf with 2 bedrooms, 2 baths, full kitchen with island, utility room, and a generous living room. The shed gets a 7-ft rear extension so both ends of the building match at 23 ft deep. These units are positioned as the premium tier in the STR mix — families and longer stays.

The look. Coastal modern across all 8 units — soft whites, white-oak accents, deep ocean blue as the signature color, brushed brass hardware, large-format porcelain tile in wet areas. Both buildings share the aesthetic so the property reads as one cohesive product.

Design Principles

Eight keys. Two tiers. One coastal campus.

  • Motel = volume — 6 studio units, high turnover
  • Duplex = premium — 2 × 2BR/2BA, longer stays
  • Diversified mix — solo travelers + families
  • Separate utilities — submeter where possible
  • Private patio per unit — pool removed

Permit note · Florida code requirement New walls between separate dwelling units are "demising walls" and require a 1-hour fire-rated assembly. Standard build: 2×4 studs with 5/8" Type X gypsum on each side, sealed at top and bottom plates with fire-rated caulk.

Door configuration · 3 doors total per unit Bedroom has its own entry door from the living room (Door 3), so the bedroom can be fully closed off for privacy. The bathroom sits inside the bedroom as an en-suite with two access doors: Door 1 from the bedroom and Door 2 directly from the living room — so common-area guests can use the bath without entering the bedroom. Recommend privacy interlock hardware on the two bathroom doors so locking one automatically secures the other.
Wet-wall efficiency The bathroom, utility room, and half-kitchen all line up along the same vertical zone, sharing a single plumbing chase along the demising wall. This streamlines rough-in plumbing compared to spreading wet rooms throughout the unit, and concentrates all the noisy mechanical equipment (W/D, tankless WH, fridge compressor) away from the bedroom and living areas.

Site Plan
Parking & patios fronting Fernwood
Pool demolished

Existing pool removed entirely. Concrete demo'd, area regraded. Frees up the rear yard for landscape and a small communal fire pit area.

Front-loaded layout

Guest sequence: pull off Fernwood → park in 2 dedicated spaces → walk through private patio → enter unit. Door, patio, and parking all line up per unit.

Private fenced patios

Each unit gets a ~10×16 ft patio with café table, lounger, café lights, and composite fence panels between neighbors. Recommend retractable awning or pergola — patios face south.

16 parking spaces total

12 motel spaces (labeled U1a/U1b through U6a/U6b) plus 4 duplex spaces (A1/A2 for Unit A, B1/B2 for Unit B) with separate driveway access on the right side of the lot.

Combined duplex zone

Right side of the lot holds the new 83-ft long duplex building (existing Unit 7 + new connector + existing shed). Shared rear yard for duplex guests, own parking, own entries facing Fernwood. Operationally independent from the motel.

Privacy hedge perimeter

'Walter's Viburnum' along rear and side property lines. Fast-growing, salt-tolerant Florida native. 6–8 ft screen within 2 seasons.

Site constraints to verify with a Bay County civil engineer 16 parking spaces, 6 motel patios, and the new ~83-ft duplex building on a 105'×168' lot is tight. Confirm minimum stall size (typically 9'×18'), drive aisle width for two-way traffic (22'–24'), impervious surface ratio (more pavement may trigger drainage requirements), fence setback rules, and that the existing Unit 7 and shed structures meet current setbacks once the connector ties them together as one building.
