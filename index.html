Vision & Concept
Two products · one lot

The opportunity. 604 Fernwood becomes a 7-unit STR campus on a single parcel: a 5-unit motel building expanded to a 93'×48' apartment building (5 × 2BR/1.5BA apartments, ~792 sf each), plus a separate 2-unit duplex created by tying the existing Unit 7 house and the existing rear shed together with a new 40-ft connector. RMF zoning permits multi-family. The straight-line main motel + the combined duplex coexist on the 0.42 acre with their own entries and parking zones.

The motel play. The existing 93'×24' motel building (5 bedroom units sharing 3 kitchens) is extended 24 ft to the rear, becoming a 93'×48' building of 5 full 2-bedroom apartments, ~792 sf each. Each apartment is self-contained — own kitchen, living room, laundry, and 1.5 baths. The kitchen sits at the front-right (existing location, existing plumbing rough-in stays put), the living room takes the front-left, and a center hall runs from the living room through the new 24-ft rear extension to a back door — with Bedroom 1 + private en-suite on the left, Bedroom 2 on the right, and the laundry tucked behind Bedroom 2. The existing motel bathroom becomes the shared full bath serving Bedroom 2 and the living room as the "half" in 1.5 baths. New gable roof replaces the existing flat roof across the full 93'×48' footprint.

The duplex play. Existing Unit 7 (19'×23' shell) and the existing shed (24'×16') are kept and tied together with a new 40'×23' connector — total ~83'×23' building, split as a mirrored side-by-side duplex. Each unit is ~955 sf with 2 bedrooms, 2 baths, full kitchen with island, utility room, and a generous living room. The shed gets a 7-ft rear extension so both ends of the building match at 23 ft deep. These units are positioned as the premium tier in the STR mix — families and longer stays.

The look. Coastal modern across all 8 units — soft whites, white-oak accents, deep ocean blue as the signature color, brushed brass hardware, large-format porcelain tile in wet areas. Both buildings share the aesthetic so the property reads as one cohesive product.

Design Principles

Six apartments. One duplex. One coastal campus.

  • 5 × 2BR/1.5BA apartments — ~792 sf each, fully self-contained
  • Duplex = premium — 2 × 2BR/2BA, longer stays
  • Building doubled in depth — 24' → 48', bedrooms in rear
  • New gable roof — replaces existing flat roof
  • Wet stacks preserved — existing fixtures kept in place

Motel · Before & After
Existing 93'×24' shared-kitchen motel → New 93'×48' apartment building

Layout transformation

The existing motel has 5 bedroom units. After renovation, the building extends 24 ft to the back (doubling its depth) and each unit becomes a full 2-bedroom apartment — own kitchen, living room, laundry, and 1.5 baths. No shared space between units.

Building expansion scope The 24-ft rear extension is 2,232 sf of new construction across the back of the building (93' wide × 24' deep). Bay County permitting will treat this as substantial new construction tying into the existing 24' deep block-and-slab shell — full structural drawings, foundation work for the new slab, framing and gable roof tie-in, and engineered review of the existing rear wall where new bedroom and back-door openings get cut.

Right-side wet stack The right exterior wall carries a continuous wet stack from front to rear: kitchen (front, existing rough-in) → existing bath (middle, kept in place) → laundry (rear new construction). Single plumbing chase reduces the labor cost of running parallel stacks. Left side carries only the en-suite bath behind Bedroom 1.
2 bedrooms · 1.5 baths · own laundry Bedroom 1 (primary, ~12'×11') has private en-suite. Bedroom 2 (larger, ~12'×15') uses the existing bath via the hall — same bath that serves the living room as a half-bath/guest powder. Net: 1 en-suite + 1 shared full bath = "1.5 baths" in real-estate terms. Family STR friendly, lower bath count than a 2/2 to keep construction scope reasonable.
Back door for rear access The center hall terminates at a back door, giving each apartment direct rear egress to the property's back yard. This satisfies typical IRC bedroom egress requirements (each bedroom has window + secondary path), supports the back yard as private outdoor space for the apartment, and provides cleaner crew access for turnovers.

Roof type · low-pitch gable The 2.5:12 pitch is on the gentle end of typical residential gables (4:12 to 9:12 is more common). This keeps the building profile low-slung and tracks the existing mid-century motel character. Sheds rain adequately for PCB climate and gives ~3 ft of attic space at the ridge — enough for HVAC ductwork, attic ventilation, and minimal storage. Wind code in Bay County requires hurricane-rated roof attachments (clips/straps) regardless of pitch.
Coastal character preserved White stucco walls, teal/turquoise shutters flanking every door and window (consistent with the existing motel's signature look), wood unit doors, and a roof color either matching the existing flat-roof gray (asphalt shingles) or upgraded to dark gray standing-seam metal (better salt-air durability, ~25% higher cost). Each unit door has its own porch light, mailbox, and house number — small details that matter for STR guest experience.
From-flat-to-gable structural consideration The existing 24-ft-deep portion has a flat roof on top of the original block walls. Going to a gable means removing the existing roof structure entirely and replacing it. The existing block walls likely need a continuous bond beam added at the top plate to handle the lateral thrust from the gable rafters. Engineering scope: structural review of existing walls, new bond beam, new ridge beam, new rafter or truss system spanning the full 48 ft depth. Standard work, not exotic, but it's the largest single structural item in the project.

Bay County permitting will require structural drawings, foundation inspections, a full building permit, and tie-in details where the new connector meets the existing Unit 7 and shed walls. Combined duplex zone

Right side of the lot holds the new 83-ft long duplex building (existing Unit 7 + new connector + existing shed). Shared rear yard for duplex guests, own parking, own entries facing Fernwood. Operationally independent from the motel.

Privacy hedge perimeter

'Walter's Viburnum' along rear and side property lines. Fast-growing, salt-tolerant Florida native. 6–8 ft screen within 2 seasons.

Site constraints to verify with a Bay County civil engineer 16 parking spaces, 6 motel patios, and the new ~83-ft duplex building on a 105'×168' lot is tight. Confirm minimum stall size (typically 9'×18'), drive aisle width for two-way traffic (22'–24'), impervious surface ratio (more pavement may trigger drainage requirements), fence setback rules, and that the existing Unit 7 and shed structures meet current setbacks once the connector ties them together as one building.
